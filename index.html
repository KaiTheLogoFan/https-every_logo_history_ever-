Every Logo History Ever!

Peppa Pig Logo History

Peppa Pig's first logo from the 2002 pilot featured rounded pink text in a wavy pattern, reflecting the show's playful tone.

From 2004 to 2025, Peppa stood on a blue background with curly-tailed lettering, making the brand instantly recognizable.

In 2025, Peppa's logo took a bold turn—removing Peppa herself and refining the cloud shape for a minimalist approach.

iCloud Logo History

The first iCloud logo, introduced in the 2011 beta, featured a brushed-metal background with a black cloud icon—Apple’s early cloud branding.

From 2011 to 2013, Apple's iCloud logo maintained a metallic aluminum look, reinforcing its sleek design philosophy.

With iOS 7, Apple shifted to a softer, gradient-based blue icon with a white cloud, aligning with the modernized UI.

Since 2014, iCloud's logo evolved into a sleek blue cloud icon with gradient depth, keeping Apple's design fresh and modern.

Flipagram Logo History

Flipagram's first and only logo, introduced in 2013, features a red circular emblem with a white play button in the center. The bold, modern typography reflects the app’s focus on dynamic content and social storytelling.

CapCut Logo History

Before launching as CapCut, the app was originally branded as Viamaker in 2020. Its logo featured intertwined rings, resembling a film reel or camera lens, symbolizing video creation.

The latest CapCut logo refines the proportions of its predecessor, maintaining a sleek, modern design while optimizing visual clarity.

Roblox Logo History

From 1989 to 2002, Interactive Physics was developed by Knowledge Revolution, serving as the foundation for Roblox's physics engine. Its tech-driven logo reflected its educational and simulation-based purpose.

The eBlocks prototype, used from 2002 to 2003, was an early experimental phase leading to Roblox. It explored block-based interactivity, laying the groundwork for the physics-driven gameplay Roblox later adopted.

In 2003, GoBlocks emerged as a refined version of eBlocks, further developing the block-based mechanics that would eventually evolve into Roblox. However, this logo did not stay for long—later in 2003, it was replaced with DynaBlocks.

From 2003 to 2004, DynaBlocks was the final prototype before Roblox’s official launch. The name emphasized dynamic block-based gameplay, which became a defining feature of the platform.

In 2004, Roblox Beta introduced the first official Roblox branding, replacing DynaBlocks. The logo featured a bold, red wordmark with a tech-inspired font, reflecting the platform’s early focus on physics-based gameplay and user-generated content. But, this logo also did not stay for long. Later in 2004, Roblox Beta finished its website, and the name officially changed to "Roblox."

From 2004 to 2005, Roblox adopted its first full logo, featuring a bold, red wordmark that solidified its brand identity. This design reflected the platform’s transition into a fully developed user-generated gaming experience.

From 2005 to 2006, Roblox refined its logo while maintaining the bold, red wordmark. This era marked the platform’s early growth, with users experimenting with physics-based building and scripting.

From 2006 to 2009, Roblox introduced a more dynamic and playful logo, featuring a bold, red wordmark with a tilted, blocky style. This era marked Roblox’s early expansion, with the platform gaining popularity among creators and players experimenting with physics-based building and scripting.

From 2007 to 2009, Roblox used this logo as a secondary variant before making it the primary logo from 2009 to 2010. The angular, stylized wordmark reflected the platform’s growing popularity and branding refinement.

From 2010 to 2015, Roblox introduced a more polished and modernized logo, featuring a bold, red wordmark with a beveled, 3D effect. This era marked Roblox’s growing popularity, with the platform expanding its user base and refining its branding to align with its evolving identity.

From 2015 to 2017, Roblox refined its logo into a sleeker, more modern design, featuring a bold, red wordmark with a slightly angular tilt. This era marked Roblox’s continued expansion, refining its branding to align with its growing user base and evolving platform features.

From 2017 to 2019, Roblox introduced a sleek, modernized logo with a distinct slanted "O," marking a shift toward a more refined brand identity. This design remained the primary logo until 2019, when it became a secondary variant used until 2022.

From 2018 to 2019, this logo was used as a secondary variant before becoming the primary logo from 2019 to 2022. The angular, slanted "O" remained a defining feature, reinforcing Roblox’s modernized branding.

From 2022 to the present, Roblox refined its logo into a sleek, minimalist design, maintaining the iconic square "O" while embracing a more professional aesthetic. This update reflects Roblox’s evolution into a global gaming and tech brand.

From 2022 to 2025, Roblox used this icon across mobile, PC, and console platforms, reinforcing its sleek and modern branding.

From 2025 to the present, Roblox refined its icon further, maintaining the square "O" while streamlining its design for a more modern and recognizable look.
